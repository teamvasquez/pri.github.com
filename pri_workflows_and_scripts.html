Follow-Up Scripts

Based on Miner's Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning (NEPQ) system. Never say "follow up" — it triggers sales resistance. Lead with curiosity, not check-ins.

NEPQ Core Principle "The single most effective way to sell anything to anyone is to be a problem finder and problem solver — not a product pusher. Get prospects to convince themselves." — Jeremy Miner
Touch 1 — After no response to your intro email (Day 2-3)
Do NOT say "just following up" or "checking in." Create curiosity and reference something real about them.
Text — warm marketTouch 1
Hey ___! I was thinking about what you mentioned about [their job / situation / goal]. Something came up that reminded me of it — I actually think it would be really relevant to what you're dealing with. Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call this week?
NEPQ principle: Reference something specific they said. This shows you were listening, not just mass-texting. The phrase "something came up" creates curiosity without pressure.
Text — cold / newer contactTouch 1
Hey ___, quick question — when we talked you mentioned [something they said]. Has that situation changed at all for you recently?
NEPQ principle: A question about their situation — not your product. This is Miner's "status quo disruption" — you're prompting them to think about whether things have gotten better or worse since you last spoke.
Touch 2 — Still no response (1 week later)
Miner calls this the "pattern interrupt." Say something unexpected that breaks the cycle of being ignored.
TextTouch 2 · Pattern interrupt
Hey ___, I'm going to be honest with you — most people in your situation tell me one of two things: either they feel like they're behind where they want to be financially, or they feel like they're doing okay but aren't really sure if their money is working as hard as it could. Which one would you say is more true for you?
NEPQ principle: This is a "situation problem question" — it gives them two honest options and invites them to self-identify. No pitch. No pressure. They feel understood, not sold to.
Touch 3 — They responded "not now" or "maybe later" (2 weeks out)
Miner's approach: never accept vague timing. Ask a future-pacing question that connects their pain to a specific future moment.
Text or callTouch 3 · Future pace
Hey ___! No rush at all — I totally get it. Quick question though: when you said "later," what would need to change in your situation for the timing to feel right?
NEPQ principle: "What would need to change" is a Miner NEPQ staple. It gets the prospect to articulate their own buying criteria — and often reveals the real objection hiding behind "not now."
Touch 4 — They said "not interested" (1 month later)
Miner's re-engage after a hard no. The goal is NOT to resell them — it's to find out if their situation has changed.
TextTouch 4 · Re-engage after no
Hey ___! I completely respect where you were when we last talked. I'm not reaching out to push anything — I just wanted to check in because things change, and sometimes what didn't make sense 6 weeks ago makes a lot of sense now. Has anything shifted for you with [their situation / job / finances] since we last spoke?
NEPQ principle: "I'm not reaching out to push anything" is a disarming opener — it lowers their guard immediately. Then the question "has anything shifted" is a low-pressure status-check that invites them to self-qualify without feeling sold to.
Touch 5 — Value share (ongoing, monthly)
No ask. Just value. Miner says most reps only reach out when they want something — be the person who shows up with value first.
Text — compound interest hookTouch 5 · Value share
Hey ___! Random but thought of you — did you know that $10k invested at 9% doubles every 8 years? So in 24 years that's $80k without adding a single dollar. Most people don't realize how powerful time in the market is vs. timing the market. Hope you're doing great! 😊
NEPQ principle: No ask at the end. The value IS the touchpoint. If they are curious, they will reply. If they don't reply, you've still stayed top of mind without being pushy.
Text — story shareTouch 5 · Value share
Hey ___! Just had a really cool conversation with a client who was in a similar spot as you — [briefly describe: paying too much for coverage / not sure if they were on track / just started investing]. Long story short, they made one small change and it's going to make a huge difference long term. Made me think of you! Hope things are going well. 😊
NEPQ principle: Stories sell better than stats. Miner calls this "social proof through narrative." It plants the seed without an ask and lets them draw their own conclusion.
Touch 6 — The "permission to move on" close (90+ days, no engagement)
Miner's reverse psychology close. Telling someone you're going to stop reaching out often triggers a response faster than any follow-up ever did.
TextTouch 6 · Permission close
Hey ___! I don't want to keep reaching out if the timing just isn't right — I totally get it, life is busy. I'm going to go ahead and let you reach back out when the time feels better for you. But before I do — is there anything about your financial situation that you're still curious about or that I could help clarify? No pressure either way. 😊
NEPQ principle: This is Miner's "takeaway close" — removing yourself creates scarcity and often prompts people to re-engage who otherwise wouldn't. End it with a question, not a statement, so there is still an open door.
NEPQ Objection Re-openers
When a prospect gave a vague objection last time — use these to reopen the conversation without repeating the same pitch.
They said: "I need to think about it"Re-opener
Hey ___! I've been thinking — when you said you wanted to think about it, what part were you most unsure about? Was it more about the timing, or something about whether it was the right fit for your situation?
NEPQ principle: Miner says "I need to think about it" always has a specific hesitation underneath it. This question surfaces the real concern without confrontation.
They said: "I can't afford it right now"Re-opener
Hey ___! Totally makes sense — and honestly I've been thinking about what you shared. Can I ask — what would need to be different financially for it to feel doable? Because sometimes there are options that work with what people actually have right now, not what they wish they had.
NEPQ principle: Don't defend the price. Ask about their criteria instead. Miner calls this "getting below the surface objection" — affordability is almost never the real issue.
Business opportunity meeting

Recruit Presentation Flow

Body language, excitement, and tonality will WIN or lose this presentation. Know this cold, then make it yours.

Before you start — your mindset: You are not selling a job. You are sharing a life-changing opportunity with someone you believe in. Your energy and conviction are your most important tools. If you don't believe it, they won't either.
1
Warm up — chit chat & small talk
2-3 min
Before anything else — be human. Ask about their life, their week, their family. This is not wasted time. It is the foundation everything else is built on.
Make it real — reference something you know about them from CRM notes
Mirror their energy — if they're relaxed, be relaxed. If they're excited, match it
Transition: "Hey, before I dive in — I'd love to hear a little more about what you've got going on..."
2
MACHO questions — learn their situation
5-7 min
Ask questions. Listen more than you talk. Their answers will shape your entire presentation — what to emphasize, what to skip, where they are most vulnerable.
• What do you do now for a living? How long have you been doing it? • Do you love it? What do you like most about it? • Are you making the money you want to make — or the money you need to make? • When do you see yourself retiring? Have you thought about that number? • What's your biggest challenge in your current role? • Do you have big goals and dreams? What does your ideal life look like? • Will your current job get you there in the time you want?
Write down their exact words — repeat them back later in the presentation
Add everything to CRM notes right after the meeting
3
Your story — make it personal and real
3-5 min
People join people, not companies. Your story is your most powerful recruiting asset — especially the brother-in-law anchor. Make it real, not rehearsed.
• Why you left what you were doing • What drew you to this (the mission, the flexibility, the income potential) • The brother-in-law anchor: "I joined my brother-in-law — he's been with Primerica for 38 years and is one of the top advisors in the company. I knew if anyone could show me the right path, it was him." • Where you are now and where you are headed
Be vulnerable — share what you were afraid of, what you almost didn't do
Connect your story back to something THEY said in the MACHO questions
4
Who is Primerica — credibility anchor
2-3 min
Keep this short and credibility-focused. They don't need a history lesson — they need to know this is real and legitimate.
• Almost 50 years in business — helping millions of middle-income families • Publicly traded on the NYSE — this is a real company with real oversight • Licensed and regulated by FINRA and state insurance departments • Mission: "We help Main Street, not Wall Street" • "Buy term and invest the rest" — the philosophy that actually works for families like yours
5
The opportunity — draw out the circles & income
5-8 min
Draw this out on paper or whiteboard — visual impact is everything. Show how income compounds as you build your team.
• Start with personal sales income: your commission on every policy and investment • Add override income: when your team sells, you earn a % of everything they produce • Draw the circles: you → your reps → their reps → growing override income • "This is how 38 years of building a team creates real financial independence" • Show the Non-captive independent structure: you build YOUR business, not theirs
Use real numbers from your upline's actual production where possible
Reference the compound interest concept — it applies to teams too, not just money
6
Connect to their goals — confirm and amplify
3-4 min
This is where you connect what you just showed them to what THEY said earlier. Use their exact words — it shows you were listening and makes it personal.
• "You mentioned earlier that [their goal / situation]. Does what I just showed you connect to that at all?" • "You said you wanted to [retire in X years / make more / have flexibility]. What I'm showing you is a path that can actually get you there." • "How many people do you know who [MACHO match — married, have kids, need more income, don't have investments]? Because those are the people we help." • "How many hours a week would you be willing to invest to actually make that happen?"
7
Handle objections calmly and directly
As needed
Expect objections. Welcome them — they mean the person is engaging. Use NEPQ-style questions to help them work through their own hesitation.
• "Is this MLM?" → "Fair question. We're a licensed financial services company on the NYSE. The model is similar to how a law firm works — you can build a team and earn from their production, but it's always built on selling real products to real clients." • "I'm burnt out / introverted" → "Actually that's one of the reasons I think this would work well for you. You get to meet new people constantly, on your terms, with a real mission behind it." • "I don't have time" → "How many hours a week would it take to change your financial future? Because most people can find 10-15 hours when the reason is big enough."
The Close — two tracks
OPPORTUNITY CLOSE: "If there's a way for you to make a lot more money, serving people in a meaningful way, and create the flexibility you said you wanted — would you be open to taking the next step?" FNA ANGLE (if they want to start as a client first): "If I put together a written financial game plan for you and you like it and use it — would you refer me to everyone you know who could benefit from the same thing?" SCHEDULE THE NEXT STEP: "Here's what I'd love to do — let's schedule a time to [complete your application / do your FNA / meet with my brother-in-law]. Would [day] or [day] work better for you?"
8
Follow-up — text, email & stories
Same day + next day
Whether they said yes, maybe, or not yet — follow up the same day. Use the NEPQ follow-up scripts from the previous tab for anyone who needs more time.
Same-day text (2-3 messages): • "Hey ___, it was so great sharing this with you today! I really enjoyed learning more about [something specific they shared]." • "Here's what I'd love to do next: [recap the next step you agreed on]." • "In the meantime — does anyone come to mind who might also benefit from seeing what I showed you tonight?" Same-day email: • Subject: "So glad we connected tonight, ___!" • Recap the conversation, their goals, the opportunity • Bullet the clear next steps • Ask for referrals in the P.S. Next-day text: • Confirm they received your email • Ask if any questions came up overnight — "Sometimes things hit you after you sleep on it"
Elevator Pitches — choose what feels natural
Financial literacy angle
"I teach people what the banks and insurance companies don't want you to know — and help them save money on taxes, insurance, and build real wealth. Do you know your financial number?"
Financial independence angle
"I help people create a financial plan to protect them from unexpected challenges and ultimately achieve financial independence — which we all want, right?"
Money game angle
"I'm in the business of helping people understand and win in the money game — debt, income protection, and retirement. Most people are losing without even knowing it."
Full coach angle
"I'm a financial coach. I educate and guide people on how to be financially free — get out of debt, protect what they've built, and achieve financial independence. Are you open to learning more?"
Primerica mission angle
"Most people think we sell life insurance and investments. But what we really sell is a better future. A better way. In Primerica, we found a better life."
Simple & powerful
"I build retirement strategies so you can stop wondering if you're going to outlive your income."
