Fast Start & Licensing

Your first 30 days, your licensing path, and the promotion road map — everything a brand-new rep needs to get moving.

Suggested agenda — for your niece
A simple 60-90 minute sit-down
  1. Belief first (10 min). Share Your Story from the Script Library — she should hear why you did this, not just how.
  2. The First 30 Days (15 min). Walk through the timeline below together — the Easy Three, the 3 field observations, the $300 bonus.
  3. Make Her List (20 min). Actually sit down and write 20-30 names together right now — don't just explain the concept. See the Start Here step on the Home page.
  4. Practice one Lead-In out loud (15 min). Pick "Know Well / Warm Market" from the Script Library and role-play it once, live.
  5. Licensing Path (10 min). Show her the order below — Life License first, then SIE → Series 6 → Series 63 — so she knows exactly what's next and when.
  6. Close with a date (5 min). Before she leaves, get her first 3 names contacted and her licensing course started — don't let momentum sit until you're free again.

Your First 30 Days

Momentum is everything early on. These are the moves that create it — do them in the first two weeks, not "eventually."

1
Submit your IBA Day 1
Independent Business Application + the one-time licensing fee. This covers your pre-licensing course, study materials, and state exam costs for your life insurance license.
2
The Easy Three Day 1-3
Contact the 3 people you're closest to — immediately. Not your biggest prospects, your closest relationships. The goal is fast momentum: a life application, a first "yes," a real conversation under your belt. This is what most Fast Start planners point to as the single highest-leverage move in week one.
3
Start your licensing course Day 1-7
Begin your state's pre-licensing course right away — most new reps who move fast finish within their first 30-45 days. Don't wait to "feel ready" — the course teaches you as you go.
4
Complete 3 field training observations First 30 days
Sit in on 3 real appointments with your trainer or upline before you run your own. This is typically tied to an early Fast Start bonus — confirm the current amount with your upline, since bonus structures are updated periodically. The real value isn't the bonus, though — it's watching a real conversation before you're the one leading it.
5
Make your list Week 1
Write down 20-30+ names — warm market, not just "hot" prospects. Don't pre-judge who will or won't be interested; that's not your decision to make for them. See the "Make Your List" step on the Home page Start Here tracker.
6
Work IN the business, then ON it Ongoing
Early on, focus on personal production — your own conversations and sales. As you get comfortable, shift more energy toward recruiting and building a team. Both matter, but production without distribution has a ceiling; distribution without production has no foundation.
Belief, Trust, Schedule, Promotion: new reps stay in motion when they believe it's possible (seeing others win), trust the structure (leaders only earn once their team earns), keep a real weekly schedule (meetings, training, appointments — same time every week), and can see a clear next promotion in front of them. If momentum stalls, it's usually one of these four that slipped.

Licensing Path

Get licensed in this order. Each step unlocks what you're legally able to sell — don't skip ahead, and don't wait around between steps either.

Step 1 · Start here
Life Insurance License
State pre-licensing course + state exam. Unlocks term life insurance — the core product every rep sells. Covered as part of your IBA fee.
→
Step 2
SIE Exam
Securities Industry Essentials — a FINRA co-requisite exam. Covers securities fundamentals. Can be studied for anytime, even before Step 1 is finished.
→
Step 3
Series 6
Requires SIE + firm sponsorship. Unlocks mutual funds, variable annuities, and 529 college savings plans — Primerica's core investment products.
→
Step 4
Series 63
Uniform state law exam. Most states require this alongside the Series 6 before you can actually place an investment trade.
Leadership level
Series 65
Investment adviser representative registration — relevant for fee-based advisory, not the typical new-rep path.
Leadership level
Series 26
Investment Company/Variable Contracts Products Principal — required to supervise other licensed reps as a branch office supervisor.
Realistic timeline: most new reps who study consistently finish Life License → SIE → Series 6 → Series 63 within their first 60-120 days. Use the practice-exam tools inside Primerica Online daily rather than cramming — a little every day beats one long session per week.
Exact fees, bonus amounts, and reimbursement rules change from time to time — always confirm current numbers with your upline or RVP rather than relying on this page for exact figures.

Promotion Road Map

A rough shape of the path ahead — exact requirements and comp percentages are set by the company and do change, so confirm current numbers with your upline. This is here to show the shape of the climb, not exact figures.

First 90 Days
Get licensed, complete your field training observations, run your first few appointments, and make your first recruiting conversations from your list.
6-12 Months
Build toward your first promotion by recruiting and helping your recruits get licensed and active. This is where "working on the business" starts to matter as much as production.
12-18 Months
Develop leaders within your own team, not just recruits — the shift from personal production to a self-sustaining, duplicating team.
Ongoing
Systems duplicate, personality doesn't — keep teaching the same fundamentals (prospect daily, run the FNA, recruit, get licensed, stay accountable) so your team can teach it too.
